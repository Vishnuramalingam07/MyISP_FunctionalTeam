Slide 2 · Executive Summary

ISP Process Journey

From Q2 FY25 baseline challenges to a mature, AI-enabled delivery engine by Q2 FY26

⚠ Q2 FY25 Baseline Challenges
Injected ~300 defects per release
Volatile scope & ad hoc releases with overlaps
Missed release schedules & delivery gaps
Last-minute surprises, overburns & reworks
Gaps in support & communication to SAs
SME knowledge gaps due to transition issues
Frequent outages and P1/P2 tickets
No common dashboards for reporting or drill-downs
No formal measurement mechanism for program capabilities
Need for predictable & consistent delivery commitments
Q3 FY25
Stabilize
  • Prod defect backlog — focused effort
  • Early Reviews: Tech Arch / UX / PO
  • People realignments — roles & responsibilities
  • Refine test processes & enforce code checks
  • Refine release process (Cal/Rollback/Reviews)
  • Streamlined handshakes — Dev & Ops
  • BA-led formal transitions initiated
Q4 FY25
Govern
  • Ticket-based SLAs, 24×5 cross-region support
  • DataDog for preventive checks
  • Roadmap alignments: PO – BA – Dev – Ops – Release
  • Established Reports & trends (Plan, Defects, Test, Performance)
  • Leadership reporting on delivery, productivity & quality
  • Ops process audit by GCC — gaps bridged
Q1 FY26
Innovate
  • Agentic skills for BA, Dev, Test & Mgmt
  • AI-DC Agent deployment & adoption
  • In-sprint regression automation / DevOps for Test
  • Shift left — prerelease metrics & DataDog alerts in lower env
  • Cross-pollinate processes to MMD & Method One
Q2 FY26
Lead 🎯
  • Publish program-specific metrics: productivity, quality, delivery
  • Measure efficiency with proactive indicators & alerts
  • Program-level trends with drill-down Leader boards
  • Scale maturity across programs
